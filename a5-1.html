II. Lamps


Ideation:


One of my most prized possessions as a child was a telescoping lightsaber toy. It was made of nested truncated cones that would telescope out to form the blade or collapse when pretending to stab your friends or enemies:

(source)


I thought this might make an interesting lamp design for a hanging lamp suspended low over my work desk. In its hanging form, it would be fully telescoped. By either lowering the cord or stacking things (e.g. books) underneath, I could put it into collapsed form, where less light could shine through.


Paper model:


There would be lots of variables to play with:

  • Thickness of each layer -- affects how much can shine through
  • Length of each chunk of the telescope -- also affects opacity
  • Angle variation -- affects how much the lamp can wiggle
  • etc.


Some ideas, time permitting:

  • Multiple telescoping tubes, of varying lengths/number of chunks, conjoined next to each other like stalactites
  • Playing with slicing settings to create different patterns on each chunks, patterns that might intersect in unexpected ways
  • It is not only a telescope but in fact a long fish 🐟


Experimentation and production plans:

  • Find out how much light passes through in the collapsed and telescoped states. Try spiralized outer layer. Print mini models with different settings.
  • Design parts to clasp onto the lamp innards
