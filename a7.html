Gina Clepper's Assignment 7!

Assignment: Propose a final project incorporating 4+ techniques from class!


Concept

I am making a box of bones toy, where you pop the bones into their proper places on the body. The pieces are press-fit in place on a wooden box, which also serves as a place to store the pieces.


Stretch goals

If time allows, I will also make squishy little organs to fit into cavities.


Sketches

The wooden box will be about 8 by 10 inches and laser cut. The pieces will be 3D printed and will have pegs on the back that fit into the board.
Stretch goals: The organs would be a combination of molded silicone and fabric pieces.


Intended Use Case

I want this to work as a toy that has ful tactile elements-- one that feels good to play with (e.g. has a satisfying press fit), is sturdy, utilizes a variety of materials, and could probably be played with nonvisually.


Context

I see these anatomical knitting models which I find so charming, and wanted to make a similar bundle of body parts that would be fun to assemble.

Task Breakdown


Rhino

I will use Rhino and Illustrator to model bones with pegs on them, as well as a press-fit box with holes in it for the pegs.

Stretch goal: I will Rhino to manipulate existing models or create my own models of select organs, e.g. a brain, and to design molds to be 3D printed and cast in silicone.


Laser cutting

I will laser cut a press-fit box. On one face of the box, I will also laser engrave the outline of a figure laser cut holes for the bone pieces to attach to.

Stretch goal: I will laser engrave another piece of wood with a torso and laser cut cavities where organs should go. This piece of wood will have box joint-like tabs to also attach to the press-fit box.


3D printing

I will 3D print flat toys representing individual bones or clusters of adjacent bones with pegs attached to their backs.

Stretch goal: I will 3D print a mold for casting silicone organs.


Interference fits

The bones will be press-fit into the holes, and the pieces of the box will be press-fit together (more snugly).


Stretch goal: Mold design

Time permitting, I will design molds for certain squishy organs, e.g. the brain, and cast them in silicone.

Contingency plans

Outsource the design of the box: use boxes.py to generate the box I need.

Abandon the box: laser cut holes in wood but don't make a hinged box, just a press-fit frame; or, worst case, just a board with holes for pegs.

Laser cut the bones: If 3D printing the bones proves problematic, one contingency plan would be to laser cut delrin. On the plus side, delrin would feel nice and smooth! On the minus side, I would need to come up with an alternative means of attachment for the bones and box.
