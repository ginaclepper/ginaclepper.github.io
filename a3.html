Gina Clepper's Assignment 3!

Assignment: 3D print shapes with specified attributes and settings. Then, 3D print 15 clips that securely connect cardboard.



Basic Shapes

Timings are ballpark:

A 2cm cube with the standard "low quality" settings:
  • Time: about 19 minutes
  • Height: 19.72mm
  • Width: 20.07 mm
  • Depth: 20.08 mm
Adhesion issues resulted in one bad corner (slightly lifted).

A 2cm cube with the standard "standard quality" settings:
  • Time: about 24 minutes
  • Height: 19.78 mm
  • Width: 19.99 mm
  • Depth: 20.02 mm
This cube turned out great!

A 2cm cube with the standard "super quality" settings:
  • Time: about 48 minutes
  • Height: 19.90 mm
  • Width: 19.99 mm
  • Depth: 19.98 mm
The back two corners were slightly lifted; I started doing between-print bed cleanings after that.

A 2 cm cube with a concentric top and bottom layer and your favorite print setting from the previous cubes:
  • Time: about 24 minutes
  • Height: 20.09 mm
  • Width: 20.08 mm
  • Depth: 20.13 mm
I used the "Draft" profile (standard quality) for this print and the following prints. I liked the look of the concentric top and bottom.

A tube 3cm in diameter and 3cm high with a single extrusion wall thickness:
  • Time: about 33 minutes
  • Height: 29.83 mm
  • Outer radius: 29.85 mm


A tube 3cm in diameter and 3cm high with a double extrusion wall thickness and random z-seam alignment:
  • Time: about 38 minutes
  • Height: 29.95 mm
  • Outer radius: 29.91 mm
The random z-seam alignment prevents the formation of a single indented seam.

A cylinder 3cm in diameter with special mode "spiralize outer contour":
  • Time: about 14 minutes
  • Height: 29.97 mm
  • Outer radius: 29.90 mm
This spiralize outer contour setting creates a vase-like shape with a bottom and a delicately thin (flexible) vase-like shape.

A cylinder 3cm in diameter printed on its side with supports on.
  • Time: about 36 minutes
  • Height: 30.07 mm
  • Radius (measured normal to print bed): 29.60 mm
  • Radius (measured parallel to print bed): 30.06 mm
I ran this print using the extra fast (0.3 mm) profile and chose tree-type supports. Less support material was needed than I expected.



Cardboard Clips

I designed some really basic clips to attach 0.114 inch thick cardboard pieces together in the same plane. I rounded the slots for easy assembly and made the slot gap slightly smaller than the measured cardboard, knowing that the cardboard had some give.
